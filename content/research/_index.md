## Interior–Atmosphere Coupling

Rocky planets begin their lives as molten worlds. As magma oceans solidify, volatiles are released into the atmosphere, fundamentally shaping the planet's early climate and surface conditions. We develop coupled models that track the exchange of heat, mass, and chemical species between the planetary interior and atmosphere.

## Magma Ocean Dynamics

Using our SPIDER interior model, we simulate the thermal and chemical evolution of global magma oceans. This includes melt dynamics, rheological transitions, volatile solubility, and redox chemistry — processes that determine whether a planet retains a thick atmosphere or loses its volatiles to space.

## Atmospheric Evolution

With JANUS, our radiative-convective atmosphere model, we compute the radiative transfer and climate evolution of early planetary atmospheres. We track the transition from steam-dominated to potentially habitable conditions, considering the roles of H₂O, CO₂, H₂, and other greenhouse gases.

## Observational Predictions

We connect our interior–atmosphere models to observable quantities — thermal emission spectra, albedo variations, and atmospheric compositions — enabling comparison with JWST observations of rocky exoplanets and future missions.

## The PROTEUS Framework

All our models are integrated into the [PROTEUS framework](https://proteus-framework.org), an open-source coupled modelling suite for simulating the formation and early evolution of terrestrial planets. PROTEUS brings together:

- **SPIDER** — Interior thermochemical evolution
- **JANUS** — Radiative-convective atmosphere
- **CALLIOPE** — Volatile outgassing chemistry
- **MORS** — Stellar evolution and high-energy radiation
- **Zalmoxis** — Escape and atmospheric loss
