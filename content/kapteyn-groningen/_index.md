## Kapteyn Astronomical Institute

The [Kapteyn Astronomical Institute](https://www.rug.nl/research/kapteyn/) is part of the Faculty of Science and Engineering at the University of Groningen. With approximately 17 faculty members, 15 postdoctoral researchers, and 60 PhD students, it is one of the leading astronomy institutes in the Netherlands.

Research at Kapteyn spans the full breadth of modern astrophysics, from the formation and evolution of galaxies to the detection and characterisation of exoplanets.

## Groningen

Groningen is a vibrant university city in the north of the Netherlands, known for its youthful population, cycling culture, and high quality of life. About one in four residents is a student, giving the city a dynamic and international atmosphere.

### Getting Here

- **By train**: Groningen is well connected by rail to Amsterdam (2.5 hours), Schiphol Airport (2.5 hours), and other Dutch cities.
- **By air**: The nearest airport is Groningen Airport Eelde (GRQ), with connections to select European destinations. Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS) is the major international airport.
- **By car**: Groningen is accessible via the A28 and A7 motorways.

### Living in Groningen

- Affordable cost of living compared to Amsterdam and other Randstad cities
- Excellent cycling infrastructure — the city regularly ranks as one of the most bike-friendly in the world
- Rich cultural scene with museums, theatres, festivals, and a lively nightlife
- International community with English widely spoken
