Tim Lichtenberg is an Assistant Professor at the Kapteyn Astronomical Institute, University of Groningen, where he leads the Forming Worlds Lab. His research focuses on understanding the formation and early evolution of rocky planets, bridging astrophysics, planetary science, and geophysics.

He develops and applies numerical models to simulate the coupled interior–atmosphere evolution of rocky planets during and after their formation, from magma ocean solidification to atmospheric emergence. His work aims to connect theoretical predictions with observations from missions like JWST.

Tim received his PhD from ETH Zürich and held positions at the University of Oxford before moving to Groningen. He is the PI of the ERC Starting Grant "MagmaWorlds" and co-leads the PROTEUS open-source modelling framework.
